Research

  • Blazar Micro-Variability

    Blazars are active galactic nuclei housing super-massive black holes that form jets of relativistic material that are pointed towards our line of sight. This gives us an unobstructed view of the inner dynamics within the jet itself with no interference from the gas and dust surrounding the SMBH. The energy emitted by these objects has shown to be variable on long-term (yearly, monthly) and short-term (days, hours) timescales. The hourly fluctuations are dubbed micro-variability and our models suggest these are caused by turbulence within the jet itself. Below is an example of data we took of 0716+71 showing such micro-variability.


    My current project is focused on the theoretical modeling of blazar micro-variability and confirming our model with observational data from our telescope and those from the SARA Consortium.

  • Ultra-Fast Spectroscopy & Valleytronics

    Transition metal dichalcogenides (TMDs) form a hexagonal lattice where conduction/valence bands naturally occur at each corner. These valleys allow for the existence of quasiparticles called excitons. My current graduate research project is on studying the coherence of excitons, and any of its excited states, in two-dimensional TMD heterostructure.


    Above is the collinear spectrometer that generates 4-pulses (4 beams) to probe the quasiparticle dynamics in 2D TMDs.
