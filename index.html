Research Interests

Short-term and Long-term Variability in Optical and Polarimetry Observations of Blazars

Fig. 1 The specific flux of blazar 2200+420 as it underwent a minor outburst and the corresponding region size in kAU.

Blazars are a sub-class of quasars who have light-years long relativistic jets of matter and light pointed very near our line of sight, and exhibit highly variable flux and polarization. My research focused on understanding the morphology of luminous outbursts to understand its energetics and physical size of the region within the relativistic jet responsible for such an outburst.

Exoplanet Detection and Characterization

Fig. 2 Image captured in 2008 of the planetary system HR 8799 which is 129 light-years away from Earth. The labeled points "b", "c", "d", and "e" are planets orbiting the central star hiden in the center of the image.

Over the last decade the number of confirmed exoplanets has skyrocketed. This has allowed astronomers unprecendented access to studying planetary systems light-years away through direct-imaging or transit light-curve observations. The research I have keen interest in is detecting exoplanets through those observational methods, understanding how the various species of exoplanets are distributed in our galaxy, and to learn their atmospheric properties through spectroscopy.
